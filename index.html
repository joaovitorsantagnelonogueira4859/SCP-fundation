Origin of the SCP foundation

The SCP Foundation is a secret organization trusted by governments around the world to contain and study abnormal individuals, entities, locations, objects, and phenomena that defy the laws of nature, which are called SCPs. Their existence is kept secret by the SCP Foundation to prevent a widespread outbreak and chaos to human civilization. When an SCP is discovered, the SCP Foundation sends agents to collect and transport the SCP to a Foundation facility or to contain it at its location of discovery if transport is not possible. Once the SCPs are contained, they are studied by Foundation scientists. Once studied, they are also categorized by threat levels according to their abilities and intentions. In which we have:

● Green:object is neither harmful nor beneficial to the Foundation, and its use is strictly regulated as detailed in the special containment procedures.
● Yellow:This object has the ability to be lethal, but this is situational or can be easily avoided, as explained in its special containment procedures.
● Orange:This object has lethality, but does not demonstrate it at all times. It may indicate that the object has the ability to be controlled under certain conditions.
● Red:This object exhibits dangerous and actively lethal properties that are harmful to humans and/or other life forms.
● Black:This object has the capability of destruction on a global scale. Furthermore, containment failures of these objects should be considered synonymous with XK-Class scenarios; their recapture/neutralization should be considered a top priority.

CSP-096

CSP-096

Real image of SCP

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-096 is to be kept in its cell, a hermetically sealed steel cube measuring 5m x 5m x 5m, at all times. Weekly checks for any cracks or holes are mandatory. No video surveillance or optical tools of any kind are permitted inside SCP-096's cell. Security personnel are to use pre-installed pressure sensors and laser detectors to ensure SCP-096's presence within the cell. SCP-096 is normally extremely docile, with pressure sensors within its cell indicating that it spends most of the day staring at the eastern wall. However, when anyone sees SCP-096's face, whether directly, via video recording, or even a photograph, SCP-096 will enter a state of considerable emotional distress. SCP-096 will cover its face with its hands and begin screaming, crying, and babbling incoherently. Approximately 1 (one) to 2 (two) minutes after first viewing, SCP-096 will begin running towards the person who saw its face (who will from this point onwards be referred to as SCP-096-1).

CSP-049

SCP-049

Real image of SCP

Special Containment Procedures: SCP-049 is contained in a Standard Security Containment Cell in Research Sector-02 of Site-19. SCP-049 is to be sedated prior to any attempted transport. During transport, SCP-049 is to be fitted with a Class III Restraint Suit (including a locking collar and extension restraints) and monitored by no fewer than two armed guards. While SCP-049 is generally cooperative with most Foundation personnel, outbursts or sudden changes in behavior are to be met with extreme force. Under no circumstances are any personnel to come into direct contact with SCP-049 during these times. In the event that SCP-049 becomes aggressive, the application of lavender (L. multifida) has been shown to produce a calming effect on the entity. Once calmed, SCP-049 will generally become compliant and return to containment without any resistance. In order to facilitate continued containment of SCP-049, the entity is to be provided with the corpse of a recently killed animal (typically a bovine or other large animal) once every two weeks for study. Corpses that become instances of SCP-049-2 are to be removed from SCP-049's containment cell and incinerated. SCP-049 is no longer permitted to interact with human beings, and requests to interact with human beings are to be denied.

CSP-1471

SCP-2317

Special Containment Procedures: All mobile devices that have SCP-1471 installed are to be confiscated and analyzed for possible leads to other potentially affected devices. Devices affected by SCP-1471 are to then have their batteries removed, be given their designation (e.g. SCP-1471-#), and then be taken to Storage Unit-91 at Research Area-45. All online app stores for mobile devices are to be monitored to prevent inadvertent sales of SCP-1471. Suspected devices are to be targeted using self-loaded malware to disable the device until it can be seized by field agents. Description: SCP-1471 is a free 9.8MB mobile app listed as "MalO ver1.0.0" on online app stores. SCP-1471 has no listed developer, and is somehow able to bypass the application approval process to directly access distribution. SCP-1471 is also able to avoid removal by other software management applications. Once SCP-1471 is installed, no icon or shortcut will be created for the application. SCP-1471 will begin sending individual images via text message every 3-6 hours. All images will contain SCP-1471-A in the background or foreground. SCP-1471-A appears as a large humanoid figure with a canine skull and black fur. For the first 24 hours after installation of SCP-1471, the mobile device will receive images taken at locations commonly frequented by the subject. After 48 hours, the mobile device will receive images taken at locations that have been recently visited by the subject. After 72 hours, the mobile device will receive images of the subject in real time with SCP-1471-A appearing near the subject. Subjects with 90 hours of exposure to these continuous images will begin to briefly visualize SCP-1471-A within their peripheral vision, reflective surfaces, or a combination of the two. Continued exposure to SCP-1471 after this point will cause irreversible, sustained visualizations of SCP-1471-A. Subjects at this stage have reported periodic attempts by SCP-1471-A to visually communicate with them, but are unable to understand or comprehend these actions. Currently, the only known treatment to reverse the effect of SCP-1471 is to eliminate the individual's visual exposure to these images before 90 hours after installation. To date, no apparent hostile activity has been reported in relation to SCP-1471-A.

CSP-106

SCP-2317

No physical interaction with SCP-106 is permitted at any time. All physical interaction must be approved by a two-thirds vote of O5-Command, and is to be restricted to experimentation only. All personnel (Research, Security, Class D, etc.) are to remain at least sixty meters away from the containment cell at all times, except in the event of a breach. SCP-106 is to be contained in a sealed enclosure composed of lead-lined steel. The enclosure will be sealed with forty layers of identical material, each layer separated by at least 36 cm of empty space. Supports between layers are to be randomly spaced. The enclosure is to be suspended at least 60 cm from any surface by an ELO-IID electromagnetic suspension system. The secondary containment area is to consist of sixteen spherical "cells", each filled with various fluids and a random arrangement of surfaces and supports. The secondary containment must be equipped with lighting systems capable of flooding the entire array with at least 80,000 lumens of light instantly without direct human involvement. Both containment areas must be monitored 24 hours a day. Any form of corrosion noticed in the primary cell, secondary cell, personnel, or any other area within two hundred meters of SCP-106 is to be reported to Site Security immediately. Any objects or personnel lost to SCP-106 are to be considered missing/KIA. Under no circumstances should any attempt be made to recover them.

CSP-343

SCP-343

SCP-343 is a male, raceless, humanoid in appearance with apparent omnipotence. SCP-343 was discovered wandering the streets of Prague and detained after a staff member saw it disappear from the streets and reappear on a rooftop. SCP-343 remains voluntarily in its chamber, as containment has proven impossible. SCP-343 remains in a 6.1 m by 6.1 m (20 ft by 20 ft) room in the Minimum Security wing of Site-17. All items requested by the entity are to be brought in, and SCP-343 is to be visited by at least one staff member each day. Attempts to add additional security precautions or required access credentials are unnecessary/impossible due to the nature of SCP-343."SCP-343, colloquially referred to as 'God' by staff here, appears to be an old man, though his features differ from view to viewer. In my first conversation with him, he claimed to be the creator of the universe. When I asked him to prove it, he laughed, walked through the wall of the chamber, and returned seconds later with a hamburger in his hand. When I returned for a second visit, the previously empty cell had been decorated by SCP-343 in an English style, complete with a roaring fireplace that appeared to be many times larger than the one outside. SCP-343 greatly enjoys talking to people and appears to be knowledgeable on all topics. Visiting SCP-343 has become a daily event for many personnel here, and all personnel report feeling happier after each visit. Attempts to block access to personnel with Level 3 clearances have been unsuccessful, as guards assigned to watch the room have left their posts, saying 'You know? He likes company' or crossing their arms when asked about it. this. Since SCP-343 has thus far been harmless, all personnel have been allowed to interact with it, and somehow all of them have free time to meet with it for as long as they need. For now, I leave this report, as interviews with SCP-343 are ongoing." - Dr. Beck
